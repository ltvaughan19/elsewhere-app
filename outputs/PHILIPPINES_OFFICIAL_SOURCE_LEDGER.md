## How to read the ledger

- **Cadence** is the maximum interval before Elsewhere should check the URL again. `Event-driven` sources also require a new check when a relevant law, circular, emergency, fee, or agency announcement changes.
- **Risk** describes the harm from publishing a wrong or stale interpretation: `critical`, `high`, `medium`, or `low`.
- **Professional gate** describes whether the future user-facing interpretation needs review by a qualified Philippine professional. `Required` means no public interpretive claim without that review. `Conditional` means direct source facts may be published after editorial verification, but personalized meaning, eligibility, exceptions, or recommendations require professional review. `No` still requires normal source verification and editorial QA.
- **Authority** uses Elsewhere's database values: `official_government`, `immigration_authority`, `embassy_consulate`, `intergovernmental`, `licensed_professional`, `reputable_institution`, `editorial`, or `community`.
- **Monitoring mode** is `automatic`, `browser_assisted`, `manual`, or `live_link`. Automatic is only a preference: sources that block safe requests, exceed the response cap, require interaction, or expose continuously changing alerts must fall back to the safer mode shown in their staging note.
- A URL in this document is a research lead, not a verified claim. `source_documents.state` remains `draft` until a reviewer approves a fresh exact snapshot.

## Non-negotiable publication rules

1. Keep **“what the source says”** separate from **“what it means for you.”** The first is a cited factual summary. The second must account for nationality, current residence, destination, trip purpose, duration, work activity, income source, family status, age, and other applicable facts.
2. A source page is not evidence of every claim on the topic. Each claim needs an exact locator, a support note, and the exact snapshot reviewed for that claim.
3. Do not present old Bureau of Immigration fee pages as current. Several BI pages disclose that fees were last updated on **2014-03-06** and may change without notice. Current fee claims stay quarantined until reconfirmed through a current official schedule or the responsible agency.
4. Do not turn live weather, earthquake, volcano, emergency, transport, or connectivity data into evergreen assurances. Link users to the current official service and show the observation time.
5. Do not imply that a national directory proves local availability, quality, price, safety, or internet performance at a specific address.
6. Legal, tax, work-rights, property-ownership, and insurance-requirement interpretations require the professional gates defined below, even when the underlying source is official.
7. If official sources conflict, change, disappear, or cannot be captured exactly, mark the source `disputed`, `superseded`, or `unavailable`; suppress dependent claims until re-review.
8. No source in this ledger may enter a public country release solely because it appears here.

## Digital Nomad Visa launch hold

**Release state: HOLD — no public availability or eligibility claim.**

Executive Order No. 86 is an official authorization for a Philippine Digital Nomad Visa framework, and one Philippine Embassy page repeats the Order's high-level conditions. As of this ledger's cutoff, the research did **not** verify final implementing guidelines, an operational government application channel, complete application instructions, a definitive income threshold, a current fee schedule, processing time, or consolidated official tax treatment.

Until all release gates below are satisfied, Elsewhere must not publish or imply that:

- applications are open;
- a particular traveler qualifies;
- a stated income threshold, fee, validity period, renewal process, or processing time is final;
- ordinary tourist status authorizes remote work;
- Digital Nomad Visa holders are automatically tax-free or outside Philippine tax obligations; or
- an unofficial form, consultant, social post, news article, or third-party application page is an authorized government channel.

Required release gates:

1. Capture a current implementation or application document from the responsible Philippine authority and verify its canonical government URL.
2. Confirm operational consistency across the Department of Foreign Affairs, Department of Justice, Department of Tourism, Bureau of Immigration, and Bureau of Internal Revenue, which Executive Order No. 86 names for implementation. Separately confirm labor-law and work-rights boundaries with the Department of Labor and Employment or qualified Philippine labor counsel.
3. Record the exact application channel, eligibility language, documentary requirements, fees, validity, renewal, work limitations, dependent rules, and effective date from current official material.
4. Obtain Philippine immigration-law review of eligibility and work-rights claims.
5. Obtain Philippine tax-professional review of all tax-residency and source-of-income interpretations.
6. Complete editorial, source-verification, mobile/desktop QA, and a final same-day availability check before publication.

## Source-document and snapshot staging template

Use one record per canonical official document or page. Do not combine multiple URLs into one source document. The ledger ID is an editorial cross-reference, not a database primary key.

```yaml
ledger_id: PH-___-___

source_document:
  country_slug: philippines              # Resolve to country_id at import
  canonical_url: https://...
  title: ""
  publisher: ""
  authority_level: official_government  # Or immigration_authority, etc.
  jurisdiction: PH
  source_language: en
  translation_status: not_needed        # not_needed | machine_draft | human_reviewed
  publication_date: null
  state: draft                           # Never pre-seed as verified
  last_checked_at: null
  last_verified_at: null
  review_due_at: null

editorial_routing:
  effective_date: null                   # Separate from publication_date; importer metadata until a dedicated DB field exists
  portal_section_slugs: []
  claim_category_slugs: []
  risk_level: high                       # low | medium | high | critical
  requires_professional_review: false
  professional_review_kind: null         # immigration | tax | property | insurance | employment | citizenship
  monitor_mode: automatic                # automatic | browser_assisted | manual | live_link
  monitor_frequency_hours: 168           # DB value for automatic checks; 24..2160
  event_driven_recheck: false            # Editorial trigger, not a source_documents field
  launch_hold: false
  applicability_dimensions:

    residences: []
    trip_purposes: []
    stay_duration: null
    work_activity: null
    household_facts: []

source_snapshot:
  source_document_id: null
  capture_method: manual                 # Hash-only monitoring does not create snapshots; url_monitor is reserved for a future controlled capture workflow
  captured_at: null
  http_status: null
  content_hash: "<64-lowercase-hex>"     # SHA-256 required
  etag: null
  last_modified_header: null

  captured_by: null

  role: primary
  exact_locator: ""                      # Section, page, heading, paragraph, table, or FAQ question
  evidence_excerpt: ""                   # Minimal, rights-safe excerpt; <= 1,000 characters
  support_note: ""                       # Exactly which claim this evidence supports—and does not support
```

`editorial_routing`, `citation_plan`, and `editorial_notes` are import-pipeline metadata, not direct `source_documents` columns. The importer must resolve slugs to IDs and fail closed when a category or section is unknown. When `requires_professional_review` is true, it must set `claims.requires_professional_review = true` (or select a category whose policy enforces it) before any claim version can enter a release; a note in this ledger is never sufficient.

### Snapshot handling

- Capture the exact bytes or normalized text used during review; keep that evidence in the private `source-evidence` store, not a public bucket.
- The evidence object must be `text/plain`, no larger than 5 MB, and stored as `<captured_by UUID>/<source_document UUID>/<64-character SHA-256>.txt`; `storage_path` is required when the snapshot is inserted.
- Record HTTP status, canonical URL after redirects, title, capture time, ETag/Last-Modified when available, and a SHA-256 content hash.
- The safe monitor stores bounded response metadata and hashes and creates a human review job; it does not create a `source_snapshot`. Controlled evidence capture creates the immutable snapshot only after a human initiates the review workflow.
- A reviewer compares the current snapshot with the last approved snapshot and records the decision. The current source-approval workflow defaults `review_due_at` to 90 days; the shorter cadences in this ledger are monitoring targets that must trigger earlier review tasks when a source changes or becomes unavailable. Do not assume the database enforces those cadences until that scheduling policy is implemented.
- Public claims cite the specific approved snapshot, not merely the current URL.
- If a source is a live dashboard or cannot be captured reliably, use a controlled human timestamped capture where appropriate and design the user experience as a live official link with the observed-at time. Never present a cached live status as current.
- Documents over 5 MB are never fed to the automatic monitor or uploaded whole to the current evidence bucket. Until a dedicated large-evidence schema and store exist, capture them manually, record the original SHA-256 and extraction provenance in the research record, map every relied-on statement to an exact page, and store only normalized text that fits the private evidence contract. Such a source remains `draft` if that provenance cannot be preserved.

## Change-control and incident rules

- A changed hash creates a review task; it never silently overwrites approved meaning.
- A redirect to a non-government domain, unexpected login/payment demand, certificate problem, repeated server error, removed document, or title mismatch triggers manual investigation.
- Critical and high-risk dependent claims are suppressed when their primary source becomes stale, unavailable, disputed, or past `review_due_at`.
- A correction records what changed, which claims/releases were affected, the previous and replacement snapshot IDs, reviewer, approval, and public correction note when users could have relied on the old information.
- Emergency information favors the live official channel over Elsewhere's cached explanation.
- The DNV hold can be removed only through an explicit reviewed decision after every DNV release gate is documented.
